GETTING TO DC and the National Mall

DC is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. You may visit washington.org to plan your trip.

Discover the world's diverse cultures at the World Cultural Festival on the National Mall with ease! Here's how to get to DC and navigate the city for an unforgettable festival experience.

![Map]

FLYING:

The DC area boasts three airports, each offering convenient access to the festival:

  • Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA): The closest airport to DC, easily reached via metro (Yellow/Blue) or rideshare.
  • Dulles International Airport (IAD): Located 24 miles west, it provides metro (Silver) and rideshare connections to the city.
  • Baltimore/Washington International Airport (BWI): Approximately 30-40 minutes north, accessible by rideshare or bus.

Airport Transfer Deal: Get to and from the airport with ease. Use Code: AIRPORTDEAL.

Pro Tip: When booking flights, consider comparing prices across these airports. Special rates from partners like Southwest Airlines offer a 5% discount on all festival-bound flights. The code is WAS usually for regional flight searches

TRAIN:

DC's Union Station is a major Amtrak hub, making it a convenient choice for travelers.

  • Especially for Northeast residents, Amtrak is a convenient, fun, and eco-conscious way to travel. Amtrak Acela and Northeast Regional lines service the East Coast corridor from DC up to Boston
  • It is centrally located within the city and is about a mile away from the 7th Street entrance (open to all audiences) of the mall (can walk of take Circulator bus).
  • Metro Red Line has a stop here for transport to other areas of the city upon arrival or there are many taxis/rideshare opportunities.

Check the live listing of all trains to Washington for scheduling.

CAR:

For DC Metro Area residents, Waze, Google Maps, and Apple Maps can help you navigate to the festival. However, we strongly recommend using rideshare or the metro to avoid traffic and parking hassles.

FARE INFORMATION

  • Metrorail fares range from $2 to $6 one-way based on distance.
  • After 9:30 p.m. and on weekends, all fares are a flat $2 for one-way trips.
  • Metrobus fares are $2 for regular routes, with a $2 transfer discount when transferring to/from Metrorail.
  • Determine your fare using Metro's trip planner, considering the date and time you plan to travel.
  • SmarTrip mobile pay is the best option to skip fare vending machine lines. Download the SmarTrip app for iOS or Android to purchase a mobile SmarTrip card or passes.
  • One-Day Passes are available for $13, allowing unlimited trips on Metrorail and Metrobus throughout the day.

METRO STATIONS CLOSE TO THE NATIONAL MALL:

  • Smithsonian (Blue/Orange/Silver Line) - Closest to the National Mall, but often very crowded during the festival.
  • Federal Triangle (Blue/Orange/Silver Line) - Located a short distance from the National Mall.
  • L'Enfant Plaza (Blue/Orange/Silver/Green/Yellow Line) - Convenient for festivalgoers, offering multiple metro line options.

Caution Advisory: Smithsonian station may be the closest, but it tends to be extremely crowded during the festival on the Mall.
